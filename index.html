research

I study causal machine learning for asynchronous, continuous-time event streams (you can think of computer system logs from operating systems, user activity logs from website, or patient records from electronic health records). My research concerns the development of computational methods for discovering causal structure from event streams and for distilling causal knowledge to other machine learning models. My research employs a wide range of methods from machine learning, causal inference, and generative models.

My research aims to facilitate the development of robust machine learning systems that can be used to support decision-making process in high-stake domains where experiments can be difficult to perform, e.g., healthcare, education, public policy, etc.
